Hariana

The Hariana cow, native to Haryana and also found in Punjab, Uttar Pradesh, and Bihar, is of medium size, featuring a white or light grey coat with a black switch on the tail. Its long, narrow face and flat forehead, along with short, stumpy horns and a small hump, are distinctive attributes. Serving as a dual-purpose breed, it yields an average of 4-6 liters of milk per day and contributes to agricultural tasks such as ploughing and carting. Its tolerance to extreme temperatures and ability to thrive on low-quality feed further enhance its appeal for dairy farming.

Ongole

Emerging from Andhra Pradesh's Ongole taluka and extending into states like Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, and Kerala, the Ongole cow is characterized by its large, muscular frame, white or light grey coat, and distinctive black switch on the tail. Known primarily for its draught capabilities, it exemplifies strength, speed, and endurance, making it an ideal choice for agricultural tasks such as ploughing and carting. Furthermore, its adaptability to various climates and terrains enhances its utility in diverse agricultural settings, underlining its importance in rural economies. Image: Semantic Scholar

Sahiwal

Originating from Punjab in Pakistan and widely distributed across Indian states like Punjab, Haryana, Uttar Pradesh, and Madhya Pradesh, the Sahiwal cow boasts a large and robust build with a reddish dun or pale red coat. Its short and stumpy horns, massive hump, and large dewlap are characteristic features. Recognized as India's premier dairy breed, it yields an average of 8-10 liters of milk daily and is revered for its longevity, fertility, and resilience to ticks and parasites. The Sahiwal cow is used for crossbreeding with other cattle breeds, such as the Australian Milking Zebu and the American Brown Swiss. Image: Wikipedia
